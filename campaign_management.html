Campaign Management

Campaign Management is a crucial process in advertising that involves planning, executing, and monitoring advertising campaigns. It typically consists of three main phases: Setup, Optimization, and Reporting.

Phases of Campaign Management

1. Setup

In this initial phase, we gather all necessary information to launch the campaign:

  • Campaign Info: Overall campaign objectives, timeline, and budget.
  • Placement Info: Where the ads will be displayed.
  • Creative Info: The actual ad content to be used.

2. Optimization

Once the campaign is live, we continuously monitor and adjust to improve performance:

  • Bidding: Adjusting bid strategies to optimize ad spend.
  • Pacing: Ensuring the budget is spent evenly over the campaign duration.
  • Targeting: Refining the audience to reach the most relevant users.
  • Modifiers: Applying adjustments based on factors like device, time of day, etc.

3. Reporting

Regular reporting helps track campaign performance and inform future strategies:

  • Weekly Delivery Reporting: Regular updates on campaign progress.
  • Weekly App Reporting: Specific reports for app-based campaigns.
  • End of Campaign Summary: Comprehensive analysis of the entire campaign performance.

Effective campaign management requires a deep understanding of these phases and the ability to adapt strategies based on real-time data and insights.
