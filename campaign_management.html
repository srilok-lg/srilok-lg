Campaign Management

Campaign Management is a comprehensive process in advertising that involves planning, executing, monitoring, optimizing, and reporting on advertising campaigns. It consists of four main phases: Setup, Monitoring, Optimization, and Reporting.

Phases of Campaign Management

1. Setup

In this initial phase, we gather and input all necessary information to launch the campaign:

  • Campaign Info: Derived from Salesforce and IO (Insertion Order), including campaign objectives, timeline, and budget.
  • Placement Info: Includes Targeting Info, IO details, and SSP (Supply-Side Platform) information.
  • Creative Info: Involves tags, pixels, and CTA (Call-to-Action) elements.
  • All this information is fed into Mosaic/Beeswax systems for campaign setup.

2. Monitoring

Once the campaign is live, we continuously monitor its performance:

  • Steller (Monitoring Dash): Provides real-time monitoring of campaign metrics.
  • Insights: Offers deeper analysis of campaign performance data.

3. Optimization

Based on monitoring data, we make adjustments to improve campaign performance:

  • Bidding: Adjusting bid strategies to optimize ad spend.
  • Pacing: Ensuring the budget is spent evenly over the campaign duration.
  • Targeting: Refining the audience to reach the most relevant users.
  • Modifiers: Applying adjustments based on factors like device, time of day, etc.

4. Reporting

Regular reporting helps track campaign performance and inform future strategies:

  • Weekly Delivery Reporting: Regular updates on campaign progress.
  • Weekly App Reporting: Specific reports for app-based campaigns.
  • End of Campaign Summary: Comprehensive analysis of the entire campaign performance.

Effective campaign management requires a deep understanding of these phases and the ability to adapt strategies based on real-time data and insights.
